Interview Strategy

Questions that reveal People Ops maturity and culture

Critical Interview Questions

Ask these to understand the company's People Ops reality:

On HRIS & Strategy:

  • "What is the company's biggest People Ops challenge right now?"
  • "Walk me through how you onboard 100 new employees per year. What's broken?"
  • "What systems are you using and why? Any plans to migrate?"
  • "How much of your People Ops is still manual?"

On Your Role:

  • "What happened with the person in this role before? Where did they go?"
  • "Who do I report to and what is their background in People?"
  • "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
  • "Do you have budget authority for hiring and People strategy, or is that above you?"

On Culture & Values:

  • "How would you describe the company culture in one sentence?"
  • "What's your Kununu/Glassdoor rating and how does leadership respond to reviews?"
  • "What's your employee turnover rate and what are people saying when they leave?"
  • "Tell me about a difficult employee relations decision leadership made recently."
Red Flags to Listen For

"We don't really use surveys" → No employee feedback mechanism

"Our HRIS is an Excel file" → Technical debt & risk

"HR is administrative" → No strategic seat

"We've never had to do severance" → Either very new company or unhealthy denial

"Turnover is just industry standard" → Rationalization of real problem

"Everyone loves it here" → Groupthink or poor feedback culture

Germany Employment Guide

Know your rights and the law

Employment Law Essentials

Contract Types: German contracts specify unlimited (unbefristet) or fixed-term (befristet). Fixed-term for 2+ years requires a "sachlicher Grund" (legal justification). Always get a written contract in German or English before accepting.

Probation: Maximum 6 months. Either party can terminate with 2 weeks' notice. Use this to evaluate if the culture/systems match what you were told.

Notice Period: Standard is 4 weeks to the 15th or end of month. Employer needs cause for termination. Wrongful termination claims must be filed within 3 weeks (tight deadline).

Severance: Not automatic. Only if contract specifies it or if employer wants immediate severance instead of notice period (Abwicklung).

Taxes & Insurance

Income Tax (Lohnsteuer): Withheld by employer. ~8-42% depending on salary and marital status (Steuerklasse). Solo = Class I, married = Class III or IV.

Social Insurance (Sozialabgaben): Employee pays ~21% (health ~8.2%, unemployment ~1.3%, pension ~9.3%, care ~1.7%). Employer pays equal amount.

Church Tax (Kirchensteuer): If registered as Catholic/Lutheran, additional 8-9% on income tax. Can exit at city registry if needed.

Solidaritätszuschlag: Additional 5.5% on income tax (phase-out for high earners). Most high salaries still pay this.

How to Read Your Payslip (Lohnabrechnung)

Bruttoentgelt (Gross Salary): Your agreed monthly salary before any deductions. Should match your contract.

Versicherungspflichtige Entgelt: Amount subject to social insurance. Usually same as gross unless you have special arrangements.

Lohnsteuer (Income Tax): Your federal income tax withholding. Depends on Steuerklasse and tax bracket. Check your Lohnsteuerabzugsmerkmale (tax card) for accuracy.

Solidaritätszuschlag: 5.5% of Lohnsteuer. Phase-out starts at €73,764 annually (2024). If you earn less, you shouldn't see this.

Kirchensteuer (Church Tax): 8-9% of Lohnsteuer if you're registered with a church. Disappears if you formally exit (Austritt) at the city registry.

Rentenversicherung (Pension): 9.3% employee contribution to German state pension (Rentenkasse). Employer pays matching amount (shown separately on some slips).

Krankenversicherung (Health Insurance): ~8.2% employee contribution. Varies slightly by insurance provider (Krankenkasse). Employer pays matching amount.

Arbeitslosenversicherung (Unemployment): 1.3% employee contribution. Mandatory. Employer pays matching amount.

Pflegeversicherung (Care Insurance): 1.7% employee contribution (higher if no children). Funds long-term care system. Employer pays matching amount.

Netto (Net Salary): Gross minus all deductions above. What hits your bank account.

Red Flags to Check: Lohnsteuer seems too high → verify Steuerklasse. Solidaritätszuschlag on low salary → error, report to HR. No Rentenversicherung → you may not be building German pension. Missing employer contributions on payslip → don't worry, they're deducted from company costs, not your pay. Netto doesn't match calculation → ask HR to explain each line.

Tax Filing (Steuererklärung)

Do You Need to File? If you're a salaried employee (Angestellter) with one employer and no other income, your employer withholds tax automatically. Filing is technically optional. But you should file if: you paid too much tax and want a refund (common), you changed jobs mid-year, you had significant deductions (home office, professional expenses), or you're self-employed/freelance (mandatory).

Timeline: Tax year runs January–December. Deadline to file is usually May 31st of the following year (or October 31st if you use a tax advisor/Steuerberater). File as soon as possible after January to get refunds faster.

How to File: Use ELSTER (online portal at elster.de) for free. Upload your annual tax statement (Jahressteuererklärung) with your payslips and any other income documents. Or use tax software like Taxfix, Steuererklarung.de, or Lohnsteuer Kompakt (€10–20). Or hire a Steuerberater (tax advisor, €150–500+) if complex.

What to Gather: All payslips from the year. Any 1099-equivalent documents (Bescheinigungen) from side income. Home office deduction estimate (€5–€1,250/year). Professional expenses (books, software, training). Donation receipts. Medical expenses over threshold (€2,600). Childcare costs if applicable.

Common Deductions for People Ops Professionals:
Commute & Travel: Entfernungspauschale (€0.30/km one-way, only days actually worked). Example: 15km commute × 220 working days × €0.30 = €990. Or claim actual BahnCard/monthly ticket costs if cheaper. No deduction if employer covers commute.

Days in Office: Track actual office days per month. Only deduct commute for days you physically went to office. Remote days = no commute deduction. Mixed arrangement? Calculate: (office days / total work days) × commute costs.

Business Travel: Conference attendance (full deduction: registration, hotel, meals). Site visits to other company locations (actual mileage at €0.30/km + hotel/meals). Client visits (same rules). Keep receipts and travel diary.

Home Office: €5/day (max €600/year) simplified, or actual costs if you can prove dedicated workspace (rent, utilities, internet pro-rata). Simplified method doesn't require receipts; actual method requires lease, utility bills, and documentation of workspace square footage.

Professional Development: HR certifications (CIPD, SHRM equivalent), coaching/training courses, conference registration, professional memberships (HRAI, etc.), books/subscriptions related to People Ops.

Work Equipment & Supplies: Laptop/tablet (depreciates over 3 years), ergonomic chair if home office, software subscriptions (Personio, Workday access, LinkedIn Premium), office furniture, notebooks, pens.

Phone & Internet: If work-issued phone: employer covers. If personal phone with business use: 20% deductible (no detailed tracking needed). Internet: 20% deductible if used for work (home office helps justify higher %).

Job Search Expenses: Recruiter fees you paid (if not reimbursed), CV writing service, LinkedIn Premium during job search, career coaching, visa/permit application fees if related to job move.

Expected Refund: Average German refund is €800–1,500 if you overpaid tax. Refunds hit your bank account 2–8 weeks after filing. If you owe money, you have until the deadline to pay (interest accrues after that).

Red Flags: If ELSTER rejects your filing → missing fields or format errors. Resubmit. If tax office (Finanzamt) requests audit → respond within 4 weeks with documentation. If you owe significant back taxes → negotiate a payment plan (Ratenzahlung) with your local Finanzamt.

Documents & SCHUFA

Track your German credentials

SCHUFA (Credit Report)

Why it matters: Landlords check this for apartment rentals. Banks for mortgages. Some employers for certain roles. A score of 500+ is considered excellent. Below 350 is risky.

How to get one: Order free Datenkopie (data copy) annually at meineschufa.de under GDPR. Takes 3-7 days. Or pay €30 for instant BonitätsAuskunft (credit report) for apartment viewings.

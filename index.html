Germany Employment Guide
Know your rights and obligations under German labor law
Working Conditions

ArbZG Working Hours

Maximum 8 hours/day, extendable to 10h if averaged to 8h over 6 months. 40h/week standard. Overtime must be compensated via time-off or pay. Mandatory 11h rest between shifts.

BUrlG Vacation (Urlaub)

Statutory minimum: 20 days/year (based on 5-day week). Market standard is 25–30 days. Unused vacation must be taken by March 31 of following year unless operationally justified. Carry-over requires employer agreement.

EFZG Sick Leave

First 6 weeks of any illness: full salary paid by employer. From week 7: ~70% statutory sick pay (Krankengeld) from health insurer. Sick note (Arbeitsunfähigkeitsbescheinigung) required from day 1 or 3 depending on contract.

KSchG Dismissal Protection

After 6 months, general dismissal protection (Kündigungsschutz) applies in companies with 10+ employees. Dismissal must be justified: operational, conduct, or personal reasons. Socially unjustified dismissal = invalid.

Probation & Notice Periods

BGB §622 Probation (Probezeit)

Standard: 6 months maximum. During probation: 2 weeks notice on either side. Collective agreements may extend to 12 months. No general dismissal protection during probation. Performance concerns must be documented.

Compensation & Taxes

Severance (Abfindung)

Not legally mandatory but typically offered in mutual termination agreements (Aufhebungsvertrag). Rule of thumb: 0.5 × monthly gross × years of service. Up to 1.0× in complex cases.
